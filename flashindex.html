Subject: English Literature

Simile
A comparison using "like" or "as" to emphasize similarities.
Metaphor
A comparison where one idea becomes another.
Allusion
A reference to something outside the work, often historical or literary.
Symbol
A concrete object representing an abstract idea.
Motif
Repetition of an image or idea throughout a work.
Mood
The emotional effect on the reader.
Tone
The speaker's attitude towards the subject of a text, developed through diction and syntax.
Imagery
The sensory details in a text,engaging as many of the five senses as possible in the description.
